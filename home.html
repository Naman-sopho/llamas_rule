No Drama Llama

Llamas are known for their gentle, mild and familiar nature. Llamas have gained cultural and economic significance in Peru since people of the Andes tamed them around 5000 years ago. This can be attributed to the fact that they are smart, helpful and relatively easy to train - perfecting tasks that they repeat a few times. Llamas are also social herd beings that foster a sense of curiosity towards human beings.

About History

Llamas of Machu Picchu

Ancient people living in the Andes tamed llamas about 5,000 years ago, since then they have become important animals in Peru. The llamas had an important place in the Inca culture, they were used as transportation, their wool was used for clothing and blankets and their meat for food.
